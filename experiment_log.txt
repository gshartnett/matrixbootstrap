ATTEMPT 12: Custom ADMM-BLAS solver — rho tuning (admm_rho_test)
===========================================================================
Implemented: custom _sdp_admm_blas that bypasses CVXPY and SCS entirely.
  - Null-space parameterization (2811-dim variable x_null)
  - Builds dense A_null = [bootstrap_tables @ N] via sparse @ dense (numpy)
    A_null shape: (27189 × 2811), ~612 MB. n_psd comes from 7 PSD blocks:
    main (142→10153 rows) + 6 charge sectors ([16,56,116,116,56,16]→17036 rows)
  - G = A_null^T A_null via BLAS DGEMM (2811×2811, cached across configs)
  - Cholesky G via scipy LAPACK cho_factor (~1s)
  - Standard 2-block ADMM with over-relaxation alpha=1.5 (same as SCS default)
  - x-update: cho_solve(F, rhs) — O(n_null^2) per step, fast
  - s-update: proj_PSD per block — eigendecomposition of each block per step
Rationale: avoids both QDLDL (slow for near-dense A^T A) and CVXPY overhead.
  Cholesky factor reused across all 7 configs (only c_null changes per config).
